00 — Before the findingsWhat I could and couldn't judge

Three things about this audit's evidence base are worth stating plainly, because they change how much weight each finding can carry.

There is no usage data, and there never can be

The brief asked me to cross-check visual prominence against click frequency. I checked: there is no analytics library in package.json — no PostHog, Mixpanel, Amplitude, GA, Hotjar, Clarity. The only event-shaped table in the schema, AuditEntry, is written exclusively by ledger-core.ts — it's the accounting hash-chain, not a usage log.

But the more important point is that heatmaps are the wrong instrument here anyway. This is a single-org internal cockpit with roughly five users, and most of these screens have exactly one user: Ameen. Recommending an A/B test at n=1 would be consultancy theatre. Every finding in Section 4 territory is therefore marked Hypothesis, and the honest validation step is not a test — it's fifteen minutes watching Ameen use this while narrating what he's looking for.

The spec covers six of these ten dashboards

BUILD_SPEC.md is the stated authority — §0: "Nothing outside this document is in scope." It contains zero occurrences of "telecaller", "arena", "my-journey", or a workshops screen. So Telecaller Pay, Arena, My Journey and GN Workshops have no stated goal I can audit against. Per your own constraint, I say so rather than invent one. I've audited their structure and left goal-alignment open, with the questions I'd need answered at the end.

I read this as the spec being stale rather than those screens being illegitimate — they look deliberately commissioned. But that's your call to confirm, not mine to assume.

Only four screens have placement language in the spec

This constrains severity honestly. The spec gives explicit placement instructions for Funnel (§8.3 "alert box at top"), Cash Health (§9.5 "Four numbers first"), Students (§7.5 "at top of the section"), and the global runway pill (§9.4, §15). Finance, Pipeline and People have no stated hierarchy. So a fold complaint on Finance cannot be graded Critical on ordering grounds — it would be general best-practice dressed up as a requirement. I've held that line throughout, which is why there are only three Criticals in this report.
