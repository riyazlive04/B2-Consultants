Product & Engineering Audit

B2 Consultants Dashboard — full-stack CRM teardown

A layer-by-layer read of the app as it exists in the repository today — onboarding through micro-interactions — benchmarked against Salesforce, HubSpot, Attio, Close and Pipedrive. Every claim below is sourced to a specific file and line.

Scope: B2-Consultants Next.js/Prisma app, Synamate-clone CRM module Method: 7 independent code-reading passes, cross-checked against docs/BUILD_SPEC.md, DESIGN_SYSTEM.md, SYNAMATE_CLONE_SPEC.md Prepared: 14 Jul 2026

Read this before the scorecard

This is not a CRM startup's product. BUILD_SPEC.md says it outright: "Multi-tenant? No — single org." This is a private, five-seat internal cockpit for one career-coaching business, with three named roles (Ameen/Admin, Karthick/Head, Asma & Nilofer/User) hardcoded around one company's workflow. The Contacts/Pipeline/Automation/Payments/Conversations module was cloned from a GoHighLevel white-label (Synamate) the founder was paying a seat for — a build-vs-buy decision to own the logic and the data, not a go-to-market bet on becoming a CRM vendor.

Grading this against Salesforce/HubSpot/Attio at Series-C due-diligence intensity is, strictly, a category mismatch — there's no orgId anywhere in the 2,384-line schema, no self-serve signup (signup is disabled by config), no billing infrastructure for tenants. I'm doing the teardown exactly as asked anyway — every layer below, scored against category leaders — because the underlying UX and architecture questions are real regardless of who's paying for a seat. But the roadmap at the end forks in two directions depending on which future is actually being built: sharpen the internal tool, or decide to productize it. Conflating the two will waste months.

Scorecard

The three-second read this app's own dashboard aspires to give its founder — applied to itself.

6. ContactsPartial

9. AutomationPartial

01

Onboarding & Provisioning

By design, thin in execution

There is no self-serve signup, and that's correct for a 5-seat internal tool — the gap is what happens in the sixty seconds after someone accepts an invite.

Current state

  • Public sign-up is hard-disabled: auth.ts:35-37 sets disableSignUp: true, with a comment stating admin-only provisioning is intentional.
  • Provisioning is invite-token based (UserInvite model, schema.prisma:1828-1841) with compare-and-set redemption to block double-accept races (invite-actions.ts:81-84).
  • A static, role-filtered App Guide page exists (app/(app)/guide/page.tsx) — reference material, not a triggered sequence.
  • Suspension is enforced at two independent layers (route guard and a better-auth session-creation hook) — unusually careful for an internal tool.

Gaps & weaknesses

  • Zero first-run tutorial, empty-state walkthrough, or setup wizard anywhere in the codebase (grepped: onboard/tutorial/walkthrough — no matches).
  • A new Head or User lands directly on the home page with no context for what the five nav groups mean or what's expected of them today.
  • No visible password-reset/forgot-password flow alongside login — recovery appears to be admin-mediated only, same as provisioning.

Upgrade path

  1. Post-invite first-touch — on first login, route new users through a 3-screen "here's what you'll do here" tied to their specific role, not the generic guide page. High impactLow effort
  2. Contextual empty states — every empty list in the app should end in one primary action, not a blank table (several currently do neither, see §7 and §11). Med impactLow effort

Top 1% benchmark

Irrelevant framing for this product's actual scope — Attio/HubSpot-style self-serve trial funnels are solving a different problem (converting anonymous signups) than this app has. The relevant comparison is internal-tool onboarding: Linear's "invite → role-scoped welcome checklist → first real object created in under 2 minutes" pattern is the right model to imitate, not a marketing-site signup flow.

02

Information Architecture & Navigation

Well-built, spec drift

The nav engine is more sophisticated than most internal tools ever get — founder-configurable, server-enforced — but it's already drifted from its own written spec.

Current state

  • Single source of truth: sections.ts defines a 25-item catalogue with role lists, merged at render with a founder-editable override stored as JSON on AppSetting — reorderable from /console without a deploy.
  • sectionAllowed() (sections.ts:159-170) is re-checked independently by requireSection() on every page and inside every mutating server action — not a UI-only gate.
  • Desktop rail collapses to icons under 1100px and persists the choice to localStorage; mobile hides it behind a hamburger drawer with scroll-lock and Esc-to-close (AppShell.tsx:71-86).
  • Active state matches DESIGN_SYSTEM §5.2 exactly: soft-blue background, accent text, 3px left accent bar.

Gaps & weaknesses

  • No breadcrumbs anywhere in the app — a zero-result grep across the whole tree.
  • Code has five roles (ADMIN/HEAD/USER/STUDENT/TUTOR, sections.ts:16-18) against BUILD_SPEC's documented three — STUDENT and TUTOR are unwritten reality.
  • Pipeline access regressed from spec: BUILD_SPEC §2.1 grants Head "View"; the live role list is ["ADMIN","USER"] (sections.ts:54) — Head is redirected out with ?denied=pipeline.
  • DESIGN_SYSTEM's flagship top-bar requirement — the runway pill "visible on every screen" — is coded as Admin-only (layout.tsx:59-65). The documentation and the build disagree.

Upgrade path

  1. Fix the Pipeline role list — a one-line change in sections.ts to match the written spec, or update the spec to match reality. Either is a five-minute fix; leaving the mismatch is the wrong outcome. High impactLow effort
  2. Add a lightweight breadcrumb/section-title pattern on nested routes (/contacts/[id], /automation/[id]) — currently the only way back is the sidebar or browser back. Med impactLow effort
  3. Reconcile the doc: either the runway pill becomes genuinely universal (a role-appropriate summary metric for Head/User), or DESIGN_SYSTEM.md stops claiming universality. Med impactLow effort

Top 1% benchmark

Attio and Linear both treat the nav rail as a live reflection of workspace state (unread counts, record badges) rather than a static list — worth stealing once there's inbox/task volume to justify it. Salesforce's App Launcher and permission-set-driven nav prove the founder-configurable-JSON approach here is directionally right; the missing piece is just breadcrumb-level "where am I" that every one of those products treats as non-negotiable past two nesting levels.

03

Dashboard / Home

Great for one role, thin for the rest

BUILD_SPEC's north star — "answers 'are we okay?' in three seconds" — is genuinely achieved, for exactly one of five roles.

Current state

  • A single server-side Promise.all (page.tsx:29-37) fetches FX rate, runway, notifications, gamification, pipeline snapshot and pending payments in one pass — no client waterfall.
  • Admin sees FounderPulse: month-pace hero, 7-day pipeline deltas, a "needs attention" list, plus a KPI grid (cash runway, pipeline value, wins this month, overdue receivables, live FX).
  • Non-Admin sees WorkTracker (a personal time tracker) instead of any business KPI — by design per the access matrix, but undocumented as a UX decision in BUILD_SPEC §1.
  • MetricCard faithfully implements the design spec: label, (i) tooltip, tabular value, delta chip with an sr-only direction label.

Gaps & weaknesses

  • No date-range picker or pipeline filter on the home page at all — every figure is hard-fixed to "this month."
  • Head sees no Pipeline-value or Wins-this-month card despite the spec entitling them to Pipeline "View" — a second symptom of the §2 access-matrix drift.
  • The runway pill, the one thing DESIGN_SYSTEM insists must be everywhere, isn't on this page for 4 of 5 roles.

Upgrade path

  1. Give Head a genuine, scoped "are we okay" view (pipeline health + their own delivery metrics) instead of the personal time-tracker substitute. High impactMed effort
  2. Add a date-range control to the KPI grid — even a simple This Month / Last Month / QTD toggle closes a real gap against every benchmark below. Med impactMed effort

Top 1% benchmark

HubSpot and Salesforce both let every role land on a dashboard scoped to them, not a binary "full view or personal tracker" split — reports and widgets are assignable per user/team by permission, not by an if/else on role. Attio's home feed treats "what changed since you last looked" as the primary object, which this dashboard doesn't attempt at all outside the Admin's "needs attention" list.

04

Permissions & Collaboration

The strongest architecture in the app

Two independently-checked, server-enforced authorization layers — sections (what you see) and capabilities (what you can do) — is more rigor than most Series A products ship. Collaboration is the weak half of this pairing.

Current state

  • sectionAllowed() and hasCapability() are pure functions, each with a per-user JSON override (User.sectionAccess, User.capabilities) layered over role defaults (rbac.ts, capabilities.ts:36-72).
  • Every *-actions.ts file re-checks its own guard independently of the page that calls it — middleware.ts deliberately does only a cookie-presence check and documents why (real checks belong server-side, not in middleware).
  • Real assignment (ContactTask.assignedToId) and a real audit trail (LeadStageHistory) exist on Contacts.
  • @mention support with notification wiring genuinely exists in the codebase — on GnPost/GnComment (the German-Note community feature), not on Contacts or Opportunities.

Gaps & weaknesses

  • The @mention pattern that already exists and already fires notifications was never extended to ContactNote — an inconsistency, not a missing capability from scratch.
  • Opportunity has no notes/tasks of its own; all collaboration data lives on the parent Lead, so a deal-specific conversation has nowhere to live.
  • Read-only *-metrics.ts helpers carry no guard of their own — safe today only because every current caller is already behind a page/action guard; a future direct caller wouldn't be.

Upgrade path

  1. Port the existing mentionedUserIds + notification pattern from GN community onto ContactNote — the hard part (notification delivery) is already built. High impactLow effort
  2. Give Opportunity its own notes, so deal-specific conversation doesn't have to overload the contact record. Med impactMed effort

Top 1% benchmark

Attio's real-time multiplayer cursors and per-record activity feed, and Salesforce's field-level security plus role hierarchy, are both a tier above what's needed here — but the specific pattern worth adopting from both is treating every record (not just Contacts) as a first-class collaboration surface with its own comment thread, not routing everything through one parent object.

05

Data Model

Correct fundamentals, one real drift risk

Money is modeled correctly everywhere it matters. The one structural risk is that "which stage is this contact in" now has two independent answers.

Current state

  • All money fields are BigInt integer minor-units (…InrMinor/…EurMinor) — zero Float money fields found, matching BUILD_SPEC §3's "never floats" rule.
  • Tag↔Lead is a real Prisma-managed many-to-many, not a string array; CustomFieldDefinition is genuine metadata-driven EAV (field defs are rows, values live in a Json blob keyed by field key).
  • No unified ActivityLog table exists — the schema itself documents that the timeline is "a read-time merge" of six different tables (schema.prisma:545-546).
  • Lead.stage (the legacy 18-value enum) and Opportunity.stageId (the new relational multi-pipeline model) coexist; a legacyStage bridge field keeps only the one seeded default pipeline in sync.

Gaps & weaknesses

  • Dual stage model is a real drift risk — any second pipeline's stage moves have no write-through to Lead.stage, so any legacy report reading Lead.stage silently misses non-default-pipeline activity.
  • No deletedAt/soft-delete convention anywhere — only ad hoc archived/active/published booleans on a few models; everything else hard-deletes.
  • InvoicePayment.recordedById is a bare, unindexed string with no FK to User — the one audit-trail field in the schema that breaks the pattern every other "who did this" field follows.
  • Zero tenant scoping anywhere (confirmed: no orgId/tenantId on any of the 74 models) — fine for the current single-org scope, a large migration if that scope ever changes.

Upgrade path

  1. Extend legacyStage write-through to every pipeline, or retire Lead.stage as a read-only projection computed from Opportunity — pick one, don't leave two ledgers of truth. High impactMed effort
  2. Give InvoicePayment.recordedById a real FK. Low impactLow effort

Top 1% benchmark

Attio's fully generic object/attribute model (any record type, any relation, no schema migration to add a field) is the ceiling here, but it's arguably over-engineering for a single-org tool. The more relevant benchmark is Pipedrive's discipline: one deal, one pipeline, one stage — no parallel legacy model left running alongside the real one.

06

Contacts & Companies

Real record, thin list tooling

The contact detail page is a genuinely good unified timeline. The list view in front of it is a client-side array with a silent ceiling.

Current state

  • Contact detail merges Notes, StageHistory, WhatsApp messages, DiscoveryOutcomes, Bookings and completed Tasks into one real sorted timeline (contacts-metrics.ts:191-261), not per-type tab silos.
  • Inline edit for owner/custom-fields/tags; modal edit for core identity fields. Companies and Tasks are full CRUD, not stubs.
  • Bulk actions exist for exactly one operation: add-tag (ContactsTable.tsx:78-98).

Gaps & weaknesses

  • Server caps the contacts fetch at LIST_CAP = 1000 (contacts-metrics.ts:33, commented "Phase 1 … server pagination is a later refinement") — beyond that, rows silently vanish from the list with no indicator.
  • Search box is a client-side substring filter over the already-fetched array; the DB-level search param on getContactsList exists but is never called by the page — dead code.
  • Exactly one filter (tag). No filter by owner, stage, source, city, or date range, despite all of those being real Lead fields. No saved views/smart lists anywhere.
  • The Opportunities tab on a contact record is view-only — no inline create or close-deal.

Upgrade path

  1. Wire the already-built search param into the page — this is a ~10-line change turning dead code into real DB search. High impactLow effort
  2. Replace the hard 1,000-row cap with real cursor pagination before the list silently starts dropping records. High impactMed effort
  3. Ship a multi-field filter panel (owner, stage, source, date) plus saved views — the single most-requested CRM primitive this product doesn't have. High impactMed effort

Top 1% benchmark

Attio's and Close's "smart list" — a saved, shareable, live-updating filter — is the single feature every category leader treats as table stakes and this product is missing entirely. HubSpot's list segmentation and Salesforce's list views both prove the same point from the enterprise end: a CRM without saved filtered views makes every user re-build the same query from memory, every day.

07

Pipeline & Deal Management

A real Kanban, running next to a ghost

This is the single most important finding in the whole audit: two separate, disconnected pipeline systems are live in production at once.

Current state

  • /opportunities is a real, working Kanban: native HTML5 drag-and-drop (no library — Board.tsx:123-165), a real stage editor (add/rename/delete via ManageBoard), and multi-pipeline support with a pipeline switcher.
  • /pipeline is the original Phase-1 dashboard: a bar chart over the legacy 15-value Lead.stage enum, click-to-view read-only modal — exactly the "read-only chart" the clone spec's own footnote describes, unchanged.
  • Stage totals on the Kanban recompute server-side on every load, not incrementally — a full board revalidate follows every card move, with no optimistic UI (opportunities-actions.ts:104-106).

Gaps & weaknesses

  • The two pipelines don't talk to each other beyond one bridge field for the single seeded default pipeline (§5) — a rep working the Kanban board and a founder reading the Pipeline dashboard can be looking at different realities.
  • The Kanban's empty state literally reads "Seed the Sales pipeline with npm run db:crm" (Board.tsx:87) — a developer setup instruction, visible in the live product.
  • No optimistic drag feedback — every card move waits for a full server round-trip before settling, which will read as laggy under real usage.
  • Stages can be added/renamed/deleted but not reordered; deleting a stage or pipeline cascades its opportunities behind only a confirm dialog, no undo.
  • No probability/weighted-forecast field anywhere in the schema — "pipeline value" is a flat sum, not a forecast.

Upgrade path

  1. Kill the dev-instruction empty state before anyone external ever sees it — a one-line copy fix. High impactLow effort
  2. Pick a direction on the split: either retire /pipeline's chart in favor of a stage-distribution view built on Opportunity, or make the legacy chart read from the same data. Two disconnected "sources of truth" for deal stage is the highest-risk finding in this whole audit. High impactMed effort
  3. Add optimistic UI to the drag interaction — move the card locally, reconcile on response. Med impactLow effort
  4. Add a probability field per stage to make "pipeline value" mean something closer to forecast. Med impactMed effort

Top 1% benchmark

Pipedrive's entire product thesis is "one visual pipeline, always accurate, forces a next action on every deal" — this app currently has the visual pipeline but not the single-source-of-truth discipline. Attio's boards use optimistic, sub-100ms card moves with server reconciliation in the background; that's the interaction-latency bar to clear once the data model is unified.

08

Search & Filtering

Doesn't exist as a product surface

There is no way, anywhere in this app, to search for something without first knowing which page it lives on.

Current state

  • Search is siloed per page — Contacts has its own box, every DataTable-based list has its own independent "Filter…" box. No shared component, no shared index.
  • Every one of those filters is a client-side substring match over an already-fully-fetched, in-memory array.

Gaps & weaknesses

  • Confirmed absent by grep across the entire tree: no cmdk, no command palette, no ⌘K/Ctrl+K handler, and cmdk is not even a package.json dependency.
  • No cross-entity search — a phone number typed anywhere can't surface the matching contact and its opportunity and its invoice in one place.
  • No keyboard shortcut to invoke search from any screen in the app.

Upgrade path

  1. One global ⌘K palette across Contacts, Opportunities, and Invoices — even a simple client-side fuzzy match over pre-fetched summaries would beat the current zero. High impactMed effort
  2. Once that exists, add recent/frequent record shortcuts — the highest-leverage addition for a founder who touches the same 20 contacts daily. Med impactLow effort

Top 1% benchmark

Attio, Linear and Close all treat ⌘K as the primary navigation method, not a nice-to-have — Close in particular is built around "never touch the mouse to find a lead." This is the single largest missing product surface identified in this entire audit, and one of the cheapest to add a first version of.

09

Automation & Workflows

A real engine, linear only

This is not a mockup — triggers fire, actions execute, waits resume. It just can't branch, and one documented trigger is wired into the UI but never actually fires.

Current state

  • Real execution: emitTrigger() creates an enrollment and synchronously runs actions inline (automation.ts:19-44); a WAIT action parks the enrollment via nextRunAt, resumed by a cron-polled /api/cron/workflows route.
  • Seven real action types (send email/SMS, add/remove tag, move stage, create task, wait) and five live trigger types (form submitted, tag added, stage changed, contact created, invoice paid) are wired into actual server actions across Contacts, Opportunities, Forms and Payments.
  • Enrollment counts (Total/Active) on the workflow list are real DB aggregates, not decoration.

Gaps & weaknesses

  • BOOKING_CREATED is a dead trigger — it's defined in the schema and selectable in the workflow-trigger UI, but no emitTrigger("BOOKING_CREATED", …) call exists anywhere in booking-actions.ts. A founder can build a "send reminder on booking" workflow today that will never once fire.
  • The builder is a top-to-bottom reorderable list, not a canvas — and the schema backs that up: actions are a flat, sequential Json array with no branch/condition field anywhere.
  • bullmq is a listed dependency and is never imported anywhere in src — every delayed step depends entirely on an external cron actually hitting the workflow route on schedule, with no in-process fallback if it doesn't.

Upgrade path

  1. Add the missing emitTrigger("BOOKING_CREATED", …) call — a single line closes a gap between what the product claims and what it does. High impactLow effort
  2. Put the already-installed BullMQ to work as the actual scheduler for WAIT steps, instead of depending entirely on external cron reliability. High impactMed effort
  3. Add one branch action type (if tag / if stage) — doesn't need a visual canvas yet, just an IF action that skips to a labeled step. Med impactHigh effort

Top 1% benchmark

HubSpot's and Salesforce Flow's visual branching canvases are the ceiling, but the more immediately relevant gap is reliability: GoHighLevel (the very product this module was cloned from) guarantees delayed actions fire via a real job queue, not a hope that an external cron is configured correctly — which is exactly the risk this app currently carries.

10

Forms & Funnels (Sites)

Forms: real. Funnels: one dimension short

Form submissions genuinely flow through to contacts, opportunities and workflows exactly as the connectivity map promised. The page builder stops at "a stack of blocks" — no rows, no columns.

Current state

  • Form submit → upsertIntakeLead (creates/updates the contact) → optional tag → optional Opportunity creation → emitTrigger("FORM_SUBMITTED"), all in one server action (forms-actions.ts:120-229) — the full connectivity chain the clone spec asked for, working.
  • Public form/funnel hosting is real, not a stub: /f/[slug] and /p/[slug]/[step] render server-side, gate on published, and record real page-view metrics.
  • Funnel builder supports 10 block types (heading, image, button, form, video, etc.) with type-specific fields, reordered via up/down buttons.

Gaps & weaknesses

  • No rows/sections/columns — blocks stack in a single vertical column only; no visual/WYSIWYG canvas, editing is a form-field panel with a separate "Preview" tab.
  • No embeddable widget/script for third-party sites — only "copy link," despite forms being designed to also embed on external pages.
  • No calendar/payment block type — booking and checkout can't be embedded directly into a funnel step.

Upgrade path

  1. Add a 2-column row container as the one missing layout primitive — closes most of the visual gap without building a full drag-drop canvas. High impactHigh effort
  2. Ship an <iframe> embed snippet for forms — a small addition that unlocks external-site lead capture, which is the whole point of a forms product. Med impactLow effort

Top 1% benchmark

HubSpot's and Unbounce's drag-drop page builders (real canvas, snap-to-grid rows/columns, live WYSIWYG) are a full tier above this — but the underlying connectivity (submission → CRM → workflow) this app already has is the harder engineering problem and it's already solved. The visual gap is real but is the cheaper of the two problems to close.

11

Payments, Invoicing & Billing

No money actually moves through this yet

The single most consequential finding in the audit: Payments is a fully-realized bookkeeping UI wrapped around zero payment infrastructure, running in parallel with — and disconnected from — the app's real double-entry ledger.

Current state

  • Invoice editor, line items from a Products catalog, tax/discount fields, PDF generation (@react-pdf/renderer), estimate→invoice conversion — all real (payments-actions.ts, InvoiceEditor.tsx).
  • "Pay online" literally says "Online payment coming soon" on the public invoice page (app/i/[token]/page.tsx:76). "Record payment" is a manual form — an admin typing in what they were told was paid.
  • By contrast, Finance's double-entry engine is real and rigorous: every income/expense write posts a balanced journal entry in the same DB transaction (finance-actions.ts:16-24), with a hash-chained audit trail and an enforced Debits=Credits invariant on /ledger.
  • Agreements (e-sign) is the most production-grade module in the app — a self-built, OTP-verified signing flow with a DB-trigger-enforced immutable seal and content/PDF hashes, not a simulation.

Gaps & weaknesses

  • No payment processor is integrated anywhere — a full-repo grep for stripe/razorpay/paypal/webhook turns up zero gateway code; the schema's InvoicePayment.provider/externalId fields are unused placeholders explicitly commented "future."
  • Payments and Finance are two disconnected books for the same event. payments-actions.ts never references the ledger — there isn't even a LedgerSourceType enum value for INVOICE/PAYMENT. An invoice marked paid in Payments produces no journal entry and never reaches the Cash Health runway math, which instead reads exclusively from Finance's own PendingPayment model.
  • Subscriptions have no billing engine behind them — the empty state admits it: "billing runs manually until a processor is wired." nextBillingDate is written but never read by any job.
  • "Send" on an invoice only flips a status flag — it doesn't email or deliver anything; the admin must manually copy a link.

Upgrade path

  1. Reconcile the two books before anything else in this section — add INVOICE/PAYMENT to LedgerSourceType and post every InvoicePayment into the real ledger in the same transaction Finance already uses as its pattern. High impactMed effort
  2. Wire "Send" to actually deliver the invoice (email, using the Resend integration that already exists — see §12). Med impactLow effort
  3. Integrate one real processor (Razorpay is the natural fit for an India-based, INR-primary business) for online payment + webhook-confirmed status — the schema already has the seam (provider/externalId) waiting for it. High impactHigh effort

Top 1% benchmark

Every category-leading CRM's payments module (HubSpot Payments, Pipedrive+Stripe, GoHighLevel itself — the very product this was cloned from) has one non-negotiable property this doesn't yet have: a webhook confirms money actually moved, and that confirmation is the only thing allowed to change an invoice's paid status. Until that exists, this is a beautifully-modeled bookkeeping form, not a payments product.

12

Conversations & Messaging Channels

Two real surprises, one mature channel, thin inbox

The clone spec's own inventory says Email is "none today" — that's now false. The bigger gap is that the inbox wrapped around these channels doesn't behave like an inbox yet.

Current state

  • Email is real: lib/email.ts:70-94 posts to the actual Resend API, fail-closed behind EMAIL_ENABLED + an API key, with every attempt logged as a Message row.
  • SMS is real: lib/sms.ts:70-96 posts to the actual Twilio API with the same fail-closed pattern.
  • WhatsApp/WATI is the most mature channel in the product — template catalog synced live from WATI, automatic opt-out on "STOP," a six-touchpoint reminder cadence with idempotency throttling, a full inbound webhook, and a reconciliation pass to correct stale delivery statuses.
  • The inbox genuinely merges Message and WhatsAppMessage into one thread list per contact (messaging-metrics.ts:19-80) — not WhatsApp-only under the hood.

Gaps & weaknesses

  • Email and SMS are send-only — no inbound webhook route exists for either, so a lead's email reply or SMS reply is never captured. Only WhatsApp is bidirectional.
  • The unified composer only sends Email/SMS — replying to a WhatsApp thread still requires leaving Conversations for the separate /whatsapp page.
  • No read/unread state, no assign-to-user field on Message, and no realtime — a new reply won't appear until the page is reloaded.

Upgrade path

  1. Add WhatsApp to the unified composer — the send function already exists (whatsapp.ts); this is UI wiring, not new backend work. High impactLow effort
  2. Add Resend and Twilio inbound webhooks, mirroring the WATI pattern that already works — this is the single change that would make Conversations a real unified inbox instead of a merged read-only view for two of three channels. High impactMed effort
  3. Add read/assignedToId fields and light polling (or SSE) for new-message detection. Med impactMed effort

Top 1% benchmark

Close's entire product identity is a merged, real-time, assignable inbox across every channel from one screen — that's the exact bar this section needs to clear. HubSpot's Conversations inbox and Intercom both treat inbound capture as the whole point of a shared inbox; a send-only "inbox" for 2 of 3 channels is a half-built version of the category's baseline feature.

Calendars & Bookings

A real self-book flow, one slot type only

The public booking page genuinely works end-to-end — it just can't do anything GoHighLevel's calendar types do, and it never tells the automation engine a booking happened.

Current state

  • A real week-grid calendar (bookings/page.tsx:190-246) with color-coded booked/open/blocked cards, plus a separate flat-list tab.
  • The public page transactionally claims a slot, upserts the lead, creates the booking, advances Lead.stage, and fires a WhatsApp confirmation — one real, working self-serve flow (booking-actions.ts:97-201).

Gaps & weaknesses

  • Only one hardcoded 30-minute "discovery call" slot type exists — no round-robin, collective, or class-style calendars, and no buffer/min-notice/max-advance window.
  • Availability is generated one date at a time by an admin — no recurring weekly availability rule.
  • AppointmentSlot.assignedToId exists in the schema but is dead — never read, set, or filtered on anywhere, so there's no real per-team-member calendar despite the column existing.
  • BOOKING_CREATED never fires (cross-referenced in §9) — a new booking cannot trigger any automation workflow today.
  • No Google/Outlook/ICS sync; timezone handling is a static IST/CET display, not actual visitor-timezone detection.

Upgrade path

  1. Fire BOOKING_CREATED from submitBooking() — same one-line fix as §9, and it unlocks every booking-triggered reminder workflow at once. High impactLow effort
  2. Add a recurring weekly-availability rule so slots don't need manual daily generation. High impactMed effort
  3. Detect visitor timezone client-side on the public booking page. Med impactLow effort

Top 1% benchmark

Calendly-grade products (and GoHighLevel's own calendar module, the direct source here) treat recurring availability, buffers, and multiple bookable event types as baseline, not advanced features. The one-slot-type, one-day-at-a-time model this app has today is roughly where most booking tools started five years ago.

Integrations

One real integration, everything else is a seam waiting to be filled

Rolling up the channel-by-channel findings above: this app has exactly one deep, bidirectional third-party integration.

Current state

  • WATI (WhatsApp) — deep, bidirectional, production-grade (§12).
  • Resend (Email) and Twilio (SMS) — real, but outbound-only (§12).
  • Per the clone-spec inventory, a Meta lead-ads webhook and a legacy FlexiFunnels intake webhook predate the CRM module and are described as already working (api/leads/) — inherited from the pre-CRM system, not independently re-verified in this pass.

Gaps & weaknesses

  • No payment processor — the single highest-value missing integration (§11).
  • No calendar sync — no Google/Outlook/ICS anywhere (§13).
  • No CRM-side webhook/HTTP action type in the automation engine (§9) — there's no way for this app to notify another system when something happens here.

Upgrade path

  1. Sequence integrations by leverage, not by what's easiest: payment processor first (unblocks real revenue tracking), calendar sync second (removes the last manual-entry step from bookings). High impactHigh effort

Top 1% benchmark

The category leaders differentiate on breadth (HubSpot/Salesforce app marketplaces, hundreds of integrations) — irrelevant at this product's current scale. The relevant benchmark is depth on the handful that matter: this app's WATI integration is already at that bar. The task is bringing payments and calendar to the same standard, not adding a marketplace.

Reporting & Analytics

Mobile Responsiveness

The pattern exists — it's just not applied everywhere

This isn't a case of no mobile design. It's a case of a correct mobile design existing in one table component and not the other.

Current state

  • The shell itself is genuinely responsive: real mobile drawer with scrim, focus trap, and Esc-to-close; desktop rail auto-collapses under 1100px.
  • DataTable.tsx implements exactly what DESIGN_SYSTEM §7 asks for — a real <table> above 720px and a stacked key-value <ul> below it, on a purpose-built tab: breakpoint. 15 files use it correctly.

Upgrade path

  1. Migrate the 17 TableShell call sites to DataTable — the correct component already exists; this is a mechanical, low-risk rollout, not new design work. High impactLow effort
  2. Give the Kanban board a real mobile mode — a single-column "my stage" view with a stage switcher, rather than horizontal scroll across five 288px columns. Med impactMed effort

Top 1% benchmark

Performance

Disciplined queries, undisciplined payload size

The server layer shows real engineering discipline. The two lists most likely to grow large are the two shipping every row to the client today.

Current state

  • No N+1 pattern found anywhere in src/server — metrics loaders consistently batch with Promise.all rather than looping awaits.
  • Bounded fetches exist and are used correctly elsewhere (e.g. WhatsApp history caps at 300/200 rows).
  • CSV export is genuinely code-split via dynamic import("papaparse") — the one lazy-loading pattern in the app, applied consistently across three components.

Gaps & weaknesses

  • Contacts ships up to 1,000 rows to a client component that then filters/sorts/paginates entirely in useMemo — real work happening in the browser that a database index should be doing.
  • The Opportunities board fetches every open/won/lost deal with no cap at all (opportunities-metrics.ts:70-82) and renders each as a plain DOM card — no windowing.
  • Zero next/image usage anywhere — 11 files use raw <img>, including the app-wide Avatar component, with no lazy-loading or automatic optimization.
  • No next/dynamic anywhere — heavy client modules (signature pad, resume editor, invoice editor) ship fully in their route's initial bundle.

Upgrade path

  1. Move Contacts filtering/sorting server-side and paginate for real — directly unblocks the search fix in §6 and §8 too. High impactMed effort
  2. Cap and paginate the Opportunities board query before a real pipeline (thousands of deals, per the original Synamate account's 10,499 opportunities) makes this page unusable. High impactMed effort
  3. Swap Avatar and other repeated <img> usages to next/image — small, mechanical, real payload win. Low impactLow effort

Top 1% benchmark

Attio's lists stay responsive into the tens of thousands of records specifically because filtering/sorting never leaves the server — this app's own metrics layer already proves the team knows how to write that query, it's just not applied to the two screens that need it most.

Accessibility

The best-executed layer in the app

This is worth saying plainly: the accessibility floor documented in DESIGN_SYSTEM §7 is largely real, not aspirational — a genuinely uncommon result for an internal tool. One interaction is a real exception.

Current state

  • Icon-only buttons are labeled by construction — the shared IconButton component requires a label prop wired to both aria-label and title; no bare unlabeled icon button was found anywhere sampled.
  • A single global :focus-visible ring (globals.css:257-261) matches the spec's 2px primary-color requirement app-wide.
  • Signal is never color-only, consistently: every status badge and delta chip pairs its color with a text label or an sr-only span — including the dashboard's up/down delta arrows.
  • Modals and confirm dialogs implement real focus-trapping, Esc-to-close, and focus restoration to the trigger element — above what most internal tools bother to build.
  • Tables are semantic <table> markup with scope="col" and aria-sort on sortable headers.

Gaps & weaknesses

  • The Kanban board is mouse-only with no fallback — cards are plain <div draggable> elements with no role, no tabIndex, no onKeyDown. Worse, the card's own edit modal doesn't expose a Stage field at all (only Status) — there is currently no keyboard-operable way to move a deal between stages, full stop.

Upgrade path

  1. Add a "Move to stage" select to the opportunity edit modal — closes the one real accessibility blocker in the app, and doubles as the optimistic-UI fix and the mobile-Kanban fix noted in §7 and §16. High impactLow effort

Top 1% benchmark

This layer is already close to the bar Salesforce (which has government/enterprise accessibility obligations) sets. The one gap — keyboard-operable drag-and-drop — is exactly the pattern Atlassian's pragmatic-drag-and-drop and most modern DnD libraries solve by pairing native drag with an explicit keyboard-triggered move menu; worth adopting that pattern rather than hand-rolling one.

Visual Design System & Micro-interactions

Faithful at the core, leaks at the edges

The motion spec is followed to the millisecond. Token discipline is strong in the shared component kit and breaks down in exactly one feature module.

Current state

  • Motion timing matches DESIGN_SYSTEM §6 exactly: 120ms hover, 160ms modal entrance, 200ms toast slide, with prefers-reduced-motion respected globally.
  • Toast and confirm dialogs run through one shared FeedbackHost singleton everywhere sampled — no page rolls its own window.confirm() or ad hoc toast.
  • The shared component kit explicitly documents why it uses text-on-accent instead of hardcoded text-white (dark-mode contrast) — the reasoning from DESIGN_SYSTEM §1.4 made it into code comments.

Gaps & weaknesses

  • The Agreements/e-sign module is the one place token discipline breaks — hardcoded text-white on hand-rolled buttons in four files (agreements/page.tsx:32, AgreementActions.tsx:112,189, AgreementForm.tsx:210) is the exact 2.72:1 dark-mode contrast failure the design doc warns about by name.
  • Off-scale type (text-lg, arbitrary text-[15px]/text-[13px]) recurs in the same Agreements pages plus daily-log and german-note — the design system's own rule ("if you're typing text-lg, you're off the scale") is violated in exactly the modules built outside the core kit components.

Upgrade path

  1. A one-pass token-compliance sweep of the Agreements module — replace hardcoded text-white with text-on-accent and off-scale sizes with the type-scale tokens. Small, mechanical, and closes the one real dark-mode contrast bug found in this audit. High impactLow effort

Top 1% benchmark

What this app already does — a written, versioned design system with contrast math done up front, and code that mostly follows it — is exactly the practice that separates Linear/Attio-caliber products from everyone else. The gap here isn't the system; it's enforcement. A Tailwind lint rule banning raw text-white/arbitrary-value classes outside the kit would have caught every violation in this section automatically.

The roadmap

Sequenced for sharpening the internal tool as it exists today — five seats, one company, GoHighLevel replacement. If the actual goal is productizing this into a sellable multi-tenant CRM, everything in "Long-Term Bets" gets reordered in front of a much bigger, unlisted item: tenant scoping across all 74 models plus a billing/self-serve layer. Decide which future this is before committing engineering time past the Quick Wins.

Quick Wins

Days · mechanical or single-line fixes
  1. Fix Head's Pipeline access to match BUILD_SPEC's documented "View" — one role-array edit in sections.ts.
  2. Wire emitTrigger("BOOKING_CREATED") — unlocks every booking-reminder workflow at once.
  3. Delete the dev-instruction empty state in the Opportunities board (Board.tsx:87) before an investor or new hire sees a npm run command in the product.
  4. Wire the dead search param into the Contacts page — turns unused code into real DB search.
  5. Add EUR-minor fields to Product/Subscription — the dual-currency convention every other money model follows is silently broken here.
  6. Migrate the 17 TableShell tables to the already-built DataTable component for real mobile support.
  7. Token-compliance sweep of the Agreements module — fixes the one real dark-mode contrast bug found.
  8. Surface overdue tasks visually — dueAt exists on ContactTask and is never rendered as a flag.

Mid-Term

Weeks · real feature work
  1. Global ⌘K search across Contacts, Opportunities and Invoices — the single largest missing surface in the product.
  2. Saved views / smart lists plus a multi-field filter panel on Contacts.
  3. Server-side pagination for Contacts and Opportunities — remove the 1,000/unbounded fetch risk before either list grows.
  4. Reconcile the two pipelines — retire or unify /pipeline's legacy chart against /opportunities's real Kanban.
  5. Make Conversations bidirectional — inbound webhooks for Resend/Twilio, WhatsApp send from the unified composer, read/unread + assignment.
  6. Put BullMQ to work as the real scheduler for delayed workflow steps instead of depending on external cron alone.
  7. Post Payments into the real ledger — add INVOICE/PAYMENT to LedgerSourceType and stop running two disconnected books.
  8. Calendar depth — recurring availability, buffers, a second bookable event type.
  9. Keyboard-operable Kanban — a "Move to stage" control as the accessible fallback to native drag-and-drop.

Long-Term Bets

Quarters · strategic, largest engineering lift
  1. A real payment processor (Razorpay/Stripe) with webhook-confirmed status — the highest-leverage single integration; today Payments cannot move money.
  2. A self-serve report/dashboard builder — every number in the app currently lives on a page an engineer had to write.
  3. Decide the multi-tenancy question on purpose — either commit to org-scoping across the schema (only worth it if the ambition is to sell this beyond B2 Consultants), or explicitly stop benchmarking the roadmap against multi-tenant SaaS platforms.
  4. A visual workflow canvas and a real row/column funnel builder — both current builders are list-based stand-ins for what was originally speced.
  5. An AI layer (lead scoring, reply drafting, next-best-action) — table stakes at the tier this audit benchmarks against now, not a differentiator.

The honest summary: this codebase has real engineering discipline in the places that are hardest to get right and easiest to skip — double-entry accounting with a hash-chained audit trail, a genuinely tamper-evident e-sign flow, server-enforced RBAC checked twice, and an accessibility floor that's mostly not aspirational. It has real gaps in the places that are easy to demo and hard to notice are shallow — search doesn't exist, two pipelines silently disagree, and the "Payments" module can't move money. None of that is a surprise for a five-person internal tool that absorbed a CRM clone on top of an accounting system in one build cycle. It is a surprise for anything being pitched at Series-C intensity — which is exactly why the first decision on the roadmap isn't a feature, it's which product this is trying to be.
